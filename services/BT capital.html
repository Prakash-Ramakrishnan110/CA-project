Long-Term vs Short-Term Capital Gains

Key differences in tax treatment based on holding period of your assets

Short-Term Capital Gains

Holding Period: Less than 12 months (24/36 months for certain assets)
Tax Rate: 15% (equities) to normal slab rates (other assets)
Indexation: Not allowed

STCG is added to your income and taxed as per your applicable slab rate for most assets.

Long-Term Capital Gains

Holding Period: 12+ months (24/36 months for certain assets)
Tax Rate: 10%-20% (with indexation benefits)
Indexation: Allowed (for non-equity assets)

LTCG on equities up to ₹1 lakh is tax-free annually. Special rates apply for different assets.

Tax-Saving Tip

Consider holding assets for the long-term to benefit from lower tax rates and indexation. For equities, the tax rate drops from 15% (STCG) to 10% (LTCG) after 12 months, and you get ₹1 lakh annual exemption.

Tax Calculation & Exemptions

Learn how to calculate your capital gains tax and explore exemptions under Sections 54, 54EC, and 54F

Common Exemptions

Section 54

Reinvest capital gains from property sale into another residential property to claim exemption.

Section 54EC

Invest in specified bonds (REC, NHAI) within 6 months to save tax on property sale gains.

Section 54F

Exemption when selling any asset (not house) and buying residential property with proceeds.
