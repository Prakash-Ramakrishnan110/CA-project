Understanding Direct Taxation

Direct taxation refers to taxes imposed directly on individuals and organizations, including income tax, corporate tax, and capital gains tax. These taxes are non-transferable and form the backbone of government revenue systems worldwide.

Government Revenue

Primary source for public services and infrastructure

Progressive System

Tax rates increase with higher income levels

Key Components of Direct Tax

1

Income Tax

Tax levied on individual earnings including salaries, business profits, and investment returns with progressive rate structures.

2

Corporate Tax

Taxes on company profits with varying rates based on business size and structure, including MAT provisions.

3

Capital Gains Tax

Taxes on profits from sale of assets, classified as short-term or long-term with different rate structures.
