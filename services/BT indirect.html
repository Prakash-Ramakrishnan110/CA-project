Indirect Taxation Framework
of India

India's indirect tax system comprises taxes levied on goods and services, where the burden can be shifted to others.

GST

Goods and Services Tax - Comprehensive indirect tax levied on manufacture, sale, and consumption

Customs Duty

Levied on imports and exports of goods with rates varying by product category

Excise Duty

Tax on goods manufactured in India (now largely subsumed under GST)

Tax Incidence Shifting

The ultimate burden of indirect taxes can be passed on through the supply chain until it reaches the end consumer.

Multi-Stage Collection

Indirect taxes are collected at multiple points in the supply chain with credit mechanisms to avoid cascading.

Regressive Nature

Indirect taxes typically represent a larger percentage of income for lower-income groups compared to higher-income groups.

Value Added Tax (VAT) in India

The consumption tax levied on goods at each stage of production and distribution until final sale.

Manufacturer Stage

Raw material purchased for ₹10,000 + VAT @4% (₹400) = ₹10,400
Finished goods sold for ₹15,000 + VAT @12.5% (₹1,875)
VAT Payable: ₹1,875 - ₹400 = ₹1,475

Wholesaler Stage

Purchased for ₹15,000 (₹1,875 VAT already paid)
Sold for ₹20,000 + VAT @12.5% (₹2,500)
VAT Payable: ₹2,500 - ₹1,875 = ₹625

Retailer Stage

Purchased for ₹20,000 (₹2,500 VAT already paid)
Sold to consumer for ₹25,000 + VAT @12.5% (₹3,125)
VAT Payable: ₹3,125 - ₹2,500 = ₹625

VAT Calculation Summary

  • Total VAT Collected: ₹3,125
  • Total VAT Credited: ₹2,500
  • Net VAT to Government: ₹625
  • Cumulative VAT Paid: ₹1,475 (Mfr) + ₹625 (Wholesale) + ₹625 (Retail) = ₹2,725

Key Characteristics

State-Level Tax: Rates and rules varied across Indian states

Cascading Effect: Partially eliminated through input tax credit

Replaced by GST: Subsumed under GST regime from July 1, 2017
