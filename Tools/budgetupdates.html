Key Budget Changes 2023-24

The Union Budget 2023-24 focuses on seven priorities dubbed 'Saptarishi' that will guide the country towards Amrit Kaal. Here are the major highlights:

Direct Tax Changes

  • New tax regime made default with revised tax slabs (0-3L: 0%, 3-6L: 5%, 6-9L: 10%, 9-12L: 15%, 12-15L: 20%, >15L: 30%)
  • Rebate limit increased to ₹7 lakh (from ₹5 lakh) under new tax regime
  • Highest surcharge rate reduced from 37% to 25% for incomes above ₹5 crore
  • Leave encashment limit for non-government employees increased to ₹25 lakh
Read More on Income Tax

Indirect Tax Changes

  • Customs duty reduced on mobile phone camera lenses and other parts to boost manufacturing
  • Basic customs duty (BCD) on compounded rubber increased from 10% to 25%
  • Excise duty on cigarettes increased by about 16%
  • GST compensation cess on certain vehicles extended till March 2024
CBIC Notifications

Corporate Tax Updates

  • Extension of period of incorporation for eligible startups for income tax benefits by 1 year (till 31.03.2024)
  • Deduction for expenditure on notified skill development projects extended till 31.03.2024
  • New co-operatives to get lower 15% tax rate (similar to new manufacturing companies)
  • Limit increased from ₹10,000 to ₹25,000 for TDS on cash withdrawal for co-operative societies
MCA Updates

Economic Measures

  • Capital expenditure outlay increased by 33% to ₹10 lakh crore (3.3% of GDP)
  • Fiscal deficit target for FY24 set at 5.9% of GDP
  • 50-year interest-free loans to states continued for one more year (₹1.3 lakh crore outlay)
  • ₹35,000 crore allocated for energy transition and net zero objectives
